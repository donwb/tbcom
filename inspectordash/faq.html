1. How accurate is a bed bug dog at detecting bed bugs, if I have an inspection will I know for certain that I have them or not?
Bed bugs dogs rate in a very high accuracy range, from 96%-98%. There is no system that can be 100% accurate in detecting bed bugs and to attempt to state so would be misleading.

2. I have a pet, will this be a problem if I want my home inspected by Dash?
We request that pets be removed from the inspection location if possible, if it is not possible to take a pet offsite, putting them into a bathroom for a short while is the next best thing. Remember a scent detection dog is still a dog. All animals in general are subject to detecting the presence of other animals.

3. If Dash alerts to bed bugs in my home, how will you eliminate them?
We are not exterminators. We detect the presence of bed bugs and report to you our findings, however an exterminator's service will be necessary if bed bugs are found.

4. I have a large office space that I need inspected. It has multiple cubicles and offices and takes up two floors in the building our company is located in. Can Dash inspect a space this size and how long will it take?
In the interest of maximizing Dash's effectiveness and alertness, we utilize a specific working formula to not exhaust a dog. Dash will work in intervals of approximately one hour scan time, a ten to fifteen minute break time and then return to scanning. This gives Dash an opportunity to rest, have a walk or tend to any other need. By working in this interval Dash can continue for between four and six hours of steady work. How long it takes to scan commercial space will be governed by many factors inclusive of access to offices, layout of the floor itself, amount of cubicles vs. offices etc.

5. Does your dog bark when he is alerting to bed bugs? I am concerned that if I have an alert the dog will make a lot of noise.
Dash is trained to alert by sitting and additional body language that a handler will be attentive to. He does not bark.

6. Can Dash detect if my neighbor has bed bugs through my walls?
Bed bugs will very often travel through electrical sockets and along wiring conduits, as well they can infest openings in baseboard molding and move under certain types of walls. Depending on the how air tight an area is that is being inspected by Dash, he may or may not be able to detect a problem on the other side of certain types of walls. If you are inspecting your apartment because you want to determine if your neighbor has bed bugs, it would not be advisable to rely on this approach to determine a problem in an apartment adjacent to your own.

7. How do I know that Dash does not have bed bugs and is not carrying them into my home?
Often times people think of bed bugs in the same fashion they think of fleas. Bed bugs do not prefer to feed off of animals because of the lack of exposure to their skin. Bed bugs would be easily visible on an animal had they hitched a ride. Whereas humans can carry a hitch hiking bed bug on their clothing, work gear, travel bags, laptop cases and any other place a bug can crawl into, dogs are not wearing clothes and nor carrying bags.

8. I am about to move into an apartment, can I have it checked for bed bugs before I move?
Absolutely. In addition you should question the management company or real estate agent involved in your move about previous problems, if any, in the apartment or building.

9. Are you exterminators? I have read articles about exterminators who have bed bug dogs where there were alleged alerts to bed bugs followed by the exterminator obtaining a treatment job.
No we are not exterminators, we are independent and have no vested interest in finding bed bugs in your home or office.

10. Can Dash search my boat?
Yes. Dash can work in almost all environments that have human use. Boats, hotels, offices, apartments, houses as well as other living and use spaces.

11. How does the handler know if Dash is tracking a bed bug scent or my cat, or even the food crumbs I dropped from my toast?
Dash is observed for his body language and alerts. He will not alert to other animals or food on the floor.

12. How do you know Dash's abilities to find bed bugs are 'up to speed'?
Dash does continual training several times a week to keep his nose and skills sharp.

13. I am an animal lover and although I know that dogs have a remarkable sense of smell I am concerned with how much a work dog works. I have heard that some canine search dogs live in cages and are only taken out to perform their duties. How do I feel good in clear conscious about a dog being used to do this kind of job?
Dogs are natural hunters. When Dash is searching for bed bugs he is doing something he enjoys to do, hunting. Dash also lives at home with his handler when not working and is absolutely one of man's best friends. When Dash is working extensive square footage sites, he is rested once an hour and even may have some play time to liven up his work experience.
